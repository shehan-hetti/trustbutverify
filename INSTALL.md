# TrustButVerify — Installation Guide

Thank you for participating in this research study! This guide will help you install the TrustButVerify browser extension on your laptop in a few minutes.

---

## Requirements

- **Google Chrome** (latest version recommended)
- The **ZIP file** provided by the researcher (`trustbutverify-v*.zip`)
- Your **Participant ID** (a unique code given to you by the researcher)

---

## Step 1 — Download and Extract

1. Download the ZIP file shared by the researcher.
2. Extract (unzip) it to a folder you can easily find, for example:
   - **Windows**: Right-click the ZIP → *Extract All…* → choose a location like `Desktop\trustbutverify`
   - **macOS**: Double-click the ZIP file — it will create a folder automatically
   - **Linux**: Right-click → *Extract Here*, or run `unzip trustbutverify-v*.zip -d trustbutverify`

> **Important:** Remember where you extracted the folder — you'll need it in Step 3. Do **not** delete or move this folder after installation.

---

## Step 2 — Enable Developer Mode

### Google Chrome

1. Open Chrome and type `chrome://extensions/` in the address bar, then press Enter.
2. In the top-right corner, toggle **Developer mode** to **ON** (the switch turns blue).

---

## Step 3 — Load the Extension

1. On the same extensions page, click the **"Load unpacked"** button (top-left area).
2. Navigate to the folder you extracted in Step 1 and **select it**.
   - Select the folder that contains `manifest.json` directly (not a parent folder).
3. The extension should now appear in your extensions list with the TrustButVerify icon.

---

## Step 4 — Register with Your Participant ID

1. Click the TrustButVerify icon in your browser toolbar.
   - If you don't see it, click the **puzzle piece** icon (🧩) in the toolbar and pin TrustButVerify.
2. A registration page will open — enter your **Participant ID** and click **Register**.
3. Once verified, you'll see the TrustButVerify dashboard. You're all set!

---

## Verify It's Working

1. Open any supported AI chat website (e.g., [ChatGPT](https://chatgpt.com), [Claude](https://claude.ai), [Gemini](https://gemini.google.com), [Grok](https://grok.com), or [DeepSeek](https://deepseek.com)).
2. Have a short conversation and copy some text from a response.
3. Click the TrustButVerify icon — you should see the conversation and copy activity logged.

---

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do I need to keep the extracted folder?

**Yes.** Chrome/Edge loads the extension directly from that folder. If you delete or move it, the extension will stop working. If this happens, just re-extract the ZIP and load it again.

### What if the extension disappears after a browser restart?

This can happen occasionally. Simply:
1. Go to `chrome://extensions/` (or `edge://extensions/`)
2. If TrustButVerify shows an error, click **"Reload"** (🔄) next to it
3. If it's gone, click **"Load unpacked"** again and re-select the folder

### What data does the extension collect?

The extension records:
- Text you **copy** from AI chat responses
- The conversation context (prompts and responses)
- Your responses to brief reflection prompts (nudges)

It does **not** record passwords, browsing history, or activity on non-AI websites. All data is transmitted securely (HTTPS) to the research server.

### Can I pause or disable the extension?

Yes — go to `chrome://extensions/` and toggle TrustButVerify **off**. Toggle it back **on** when you're ready to resume.

### Who do I contact if I have issues?

Contact the researcher who provided your Participant ID.

---

## Uninstalling

When the study is complete:
1. Go to `chrome://extensions/` (or `edge://extensions/`)
2. Find TrustButVerify and click **Remove**
3. Delete the extracted folder from your computer
